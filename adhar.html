Know what is Aadhar card

An Aadhaar card is a 12-digit unique identification number issued by the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI), a government agency of India. It serves as a proof of identity and address for Indian citizens. The term "Aadhaar" is a Hindi word that means "foundation" or "support," signifying its role as a foundational identification document.

Eligiblity

Resident of India:
You must be a resident of India to be eligible for an Aadhaar card. Indian citizenship is not a mandatory requirement. Non-resident Indians (NRIs), as well as foreign nationals residing in India, can also apply for an Aadhaar card if they meet certain conditions.
Age Requirement:
There is no minimum age requirement to apply for an Aadhaar card. Even children and infants can have Aadhaar cards. Parents or legal guardians can apply for Aadhaar cards on behalf of their children.
Document Verification:
You must provide valid documents as proof of identity, proof of address, and proof of date of birth. These documents may include a passport, voter ID, ration card, driver's license, utility bills, and other government-issued documents. The specific requirements may vary based on your individual circumstances.
Biometric Data:
As part of the Aadhaar enrollment process, your biometric data, including fingerprints and retina scans, will be collected. This data helps ensure the uniqueness of the Aadhaar number.
No Duplicate Aadhaar:
It is not permissible for an individual to have multiple Aadhaar numbers. If you already have an Aadhaar card, you should not apply for a new one.
Voluntary Enrollment:
Enrollment for Aadhaar is voluntary, not mandatory, except in specific cases where it may be required by the government for certain benefits and services.

Application procedure

Offline

  1. Visit Nearest Aadhaar Enrollment Center
  2. Fill Enrollment Form with Accurate Info
  3. Verify Identity and Address Documents (Originals + Copies)
  4. Capture Biometric Data (Fingerprints, Retina) Take Photograph
  5. Review and Correct Data if Needed
  6. Receive Acknowledgment Slip (Contains Enrollment Number)

Online

  • Go to UIDAI Website: Visit https://uidai.gov.in/.
  • Click "Aadhaar Enrolment" or "Get Aadhaar".
  • Fill out form with personal info.
  • Book appointment at chosen center.
  • Gather required documents.
  • Visit Enrollment Center.
